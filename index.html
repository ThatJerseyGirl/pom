About Me

I’m a student developer, visual artist, writer, and advocate with a passion for turning lived experience into purpose-driven work.

My path into tech didn’t begin in a traditional classroom or career track. It began with curiosity. I discovered how much I enjoyed creating custom GPT bots and personalizing my blog with HTML. What started as a creative experiment quickly became something deeper: a new language, a new outlet, and a new future.

Before pursuing development, my work history was in restaurants and retail. After years of living with disability and navigating the realities of using a walker and wheelchair, I knew I needed a career path that could grow with me rather than work against me. Coding opened that door.

What I love most about development is the way it feels like both a puzzle and a creative process. Writing HTML feels like building the structure of an idea. CSS lets me shape it, style it, and bring beauty to the screen. JavaScript adds movement, interaction, and possibility. I love putting on music, getting into a focused flow, and watching lines of code become something real.

My journey has also shaped the kind of developer I want to become. As a survivor who has moved from victim to victor, I understand the power of voice, access, and digital spaces. Through healing, faith, inner strength, and the support of organizations like Reset180, I reclaimed my identity and found the courage to build a life rooted in purpose.

As I continue learning and growing in tech, I bring more than technical skills to the table. I bring resilience, creativity, empathy, and a deep commitment to using technology for connection, advocacy, and meaningful change.

I’m currently building my skills in HTML, CSS, JavaScript, blog customization, and GPT development, with a focus on creating digital tools and experiences that are thoughtful, accessible, and human-centered.
